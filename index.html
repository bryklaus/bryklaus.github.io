Biography

I received my B.A. in History in 2009 from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, with a senior honors thesis on the transformation of Lacanian psychoanalysis from a clinical discourse to a theoretical approach in the humanities. The thesis was awarded the Arthur Fondiler Award for Best History Thesis as well as the Robert Hayden Humanities Award for Best Honors Thesis.

In 2016, I completed my Ph.D. in German at Johns Hopkins University, with a dissertation on form, life, and the materiality of writing around 1800. While completing my Ph.D., I was also a visiting doctoral researcher at the Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin (2013) and the Freie Universität Berlin (2013–2014, 2016).

From 2016 to 2018, I was a Visiting Assistant Professor of German at Occidental College in Los Angeles, CA.

Since 2018, I am Assistant Professor of German at Virginia Tech in Blacksburg, VA.

My research and teaching focus on the intersection of literature and science in eighteenth and nineteenth-century Germany.

Digital Humanities

Editor, Goethe-Lexicon of Philosophical Concepts : The Goethe-Lexicon of Philosophical Concepts ( GLPC ) is an international research initiative investigating the central role played by concepts in Goethe’s development as a philosopher. As a dynamic reference work that will produce more than 300 entries over the next decade, the GLPC will help literary and cultural critics, philosophers, and scholars working in the digital humanities map Goethe’s philosophical heterodoxies.
